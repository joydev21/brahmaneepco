BACKGROUND OF THE PROJECT

Brahmaputra Board, in its endeavour to find a long term practical solution to the perennial flood and erosion problem of the mighty Brahmaputra, is collaborating with the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati (IITG), one of the most prestigious Institutes of our country, situated on the bank of it. Before finalizing the project, a number of discussions/meetings/deliberations were held with eminent scientists/engineers working in various fields of water resources in various Central Government/State Government and other Organisations including those from IITG, National Institute of Hydrology (NIH), North-East Space Application Centre (NESAC), North Eastern Regional Institute of Water and Land Management (NERIWALM), Guwahati University (GU), Assam Engineering College (AEC), ASTEC, IMD, Inland Waterways Authority of India(IWAI), Water Resources Department, Govt of Assam(WRD), State Disaster Management Authority ( SDMA), Assam, Tea Board of India, College of Science and Technology (Bhutan), North East Regional Institute of Science and Technology (NERIST), Itanagar, Arunachal Pradesh, Central Water Commission (CWC) and Brahmaputra Board. Valuable suggestions/views received through these are considered to formulate the project proposal and accordingly this collaborative project “Mathematical Model study of River Brahmaputra with Emphasis on Climate Change” is being taken up with a focus on assessment of climate change impact on Brahmaputra River. Prof. A. K. Sarma of IITG, as Principal Investigator, a group of Co-Investigators and project staff (given in the working group) are working for the model development so that a generalized model can be developed, calibrated and validated with support of engineers of Brahmaputra Board and can be utilized for various purposes like mitigation of flood & erosion hazard and better water utilization.
